Dr. Winston Chow

                            
                                Associate Professor of Science, Technology and Society, 

                                College of Integrative Studies, 

                                Singapore Management University

                            
                        

                        

                            
                            

                                Winston Chow is Associate Professor of Science, Technology, and Society at SMU’s College of Integrative Studies and School of Social Sciences. He researches on topics relating from urban vulnerability to climate change, urban heat island adaptation and mitigation, and environmental perceptions in tourism. He is a Principal Investigator for the Cooling Singapore initiative, and is an Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change Lead Author for their chapter on “Cities, Settlements and Key Infrastructure” in the Sixth Assessment Report on Climate Change. He enjoys talking, teaching, and writing about these research issues, and he tweets sporadically on many topics (unrelated to climate change) at @winstontlchow. 
                            

                        

                    

                    

                        
                        

                            
Dr. Justin Tse

                            
                                Assistant Professor of Humanities (Education), 

                                College of Integrative Studies, 

                                Singapore Management University
                            
                        

                        

                            
                            

                                Justin Tse is Assistant Professor of Humanities (Education) in the College of Integrative Studies and the School of Social Sciences at Singapore Management University. He was lead editor of Theological Reflections on the Hong Kong Umbrella Movement (Palgrave, 2016) and is working on the manuscript The Secular as Sheets of Scattered Sand: Cantonese Protestants and Pacific Secularities (in preliminary agreement, University of Notre Dame Press). In addition to Big Questions, he teaches a module titled Publics and Privates on the Pacific Rim.

Mr. Szue Hann Tan

                            
                                Chairman, Sustainability 

                                Singapore Institute of Architects 
                            
                        

                        

                            
                            

                                Tan Szue Hann is an award-winning architect, strategist and thought leader in sustainability and sustainable development. Hann is the Chairman of Sustainability, Singapore Institute of Architects, the professional body for Architects in Singapore, and leads sustainable development at a leading global hospitality chain. Hann is also an appointed World Cities Summit Young Leader, and BCA Built Environment Young Leader. He has spoken in numerous conferences including SWITCH, the UN Asia Pacific Urban Forum, and COP25 in Madrid.

Dr. Emily Soon

                            
                                Lecturer of Humanities 

                                Office of Core Curriculum and School of Social Sciences 

                                Singapore Management University
                            
                        

                        

                            
                            

                                Emily Soon is Lecturer of Humanities in the Office of Core Curriculum and School of Social Sciences at Singapore Management University. Her research focuses on cross-cultural literary engagement between Asia and Europe in the premodern and modern eras. Her work on cultural inclusivity and student Shakespeare performances in late-colonial Singapore has been published in Shakespeare Survey, and her research on early modern literary exchange is forthcoming in Modern Philology and England’s Asian Renaissance. In addition to Big Questions, she also teaches the modules ‘Asia and World Literature: Beyond Orientalism’ and ‘Imagining the Self: Literature, Ethnicity and Gender in Asia’.

Mr Augustine Tan

                            
                                Founder, UglyFood
                            
                        

                        

                            
                            

                                 Uglyfood tackles the surplus food in the ecosystem , we aim to fix misconceptions around edible food perceived as being inedible. Food waste is one of the biggest waste streams in Singapore and the amount of food waste generated has grown by around 20% over the last 10 years. In 2019, Singapore generated around 744 million kg of food waste. That is equivalent to 2 bowls of rice per person per day, or around 51,000 double decker buses.

Mr. Jaime Ho

                            
                                Partner, Finsbury Glover Hering
                            
                        

                        

                            
                            

                                Following a career of nearly 25 years in diplomacy and journalism, Jaime joined Finsbury Glover Hering, a leading global strategic communications and public affairs communications consultancy, in June 2022.

                                As a diplomat in Singapore’s foreign service, he oversaw issues ranging from trade to climate change, including bilateral relations with the United States, Europe, Malaysia and Indonesia. He has also served overseas in Washington, D.C. and Geneva. His last assignment as a diplomat was as a senior negotiator representing Singapore in international climate negotiations.

                                His nearly nine-year career in journalism at Mediacorp, Singapore’s national media network, saw him lead TV, radio and digital newsrooms. As Chief Editor of CNA Digital from 2016 to 2022, he led the growth of CNA to become the primary digital news source in Singapore. He was the host of more than 50 episodes of CNA’s podcast The Climate Conversations. 
                            

                        

                    

                    

                        
                        

                            
Dr. Darlene Machell de Leon Espena

                            
                                Assistant Professor of Humanities (Education), 

                                College of Integrative Studies, 

                                Singapore Management University
                            
                        

                        

                            
                            

                                Darlene Machell Espena is Assistant Professor of Humanities at the College of Integrative Studies, Singapore Management University (SMU), where she has taught courses on International Relations on Film, Film in Southeast Asia, Cultural History of the Cold War in Asia, and Big Questions. She earned her PhD in History (2017) and MSc. in Asian Studies (2012) from Nanyang Technological University (NTU). Her research includes cinema, dance, and politics in postcolonial Southeast Asia, cultural history of the Cold War in Southeast Asia, and cultural discourses on education in Singapore. Before joining SMU in August 2018, she was a Research Fellow at Singapore’s National Institute of Education (NIE). She has held teaching positions at De La Salle University and Ateneo de Manila University in the Philippines. Her current book project is entitled “Heralding the Nation: Cinema and Politics in Postcolonial Southeast Asia.

Dr. Aidan Wong

                            
                                Assistant Professor of Humanities (Education), 

                                College of Integrative Studies, 

                                Singapore Management University

                            
                        

                        

                            
                            

                                Aidan Wong is the Assistant Professor of Humanities in the College of Integrative Studies & the School of Social Sciences at Singapore Management University. Trained as a Geographer, Aidan’s research interests include debates on the politics of heritage, global economic change and public geographies. In addition to this, Aidan has been researching on waste and the politics and practices of electronic waste management for more than a decade, and is more recently engaged in qualifying and quantifying the impact of the extended producer responsibility framework in Singapore. Within SMU, he has researched on the impact of online teaching and learning on the core curriculum, particularly at how better collaboration, inclusivity and scalability can be adopted.

Joline Tang

                            
                                Founder, 

                                Sustainability Project
                            
                        

                        

                            
                            

                                Joline Tang has been passionate about sustainability and the conservation of the environment, since 2011. During her school term in SMU, she was the Vice president of SMU’s environment club, which allowed her to drive campaigns, meet like-minded individuals and build sustainability in SMU. She is highly involved in the sustainability industry through her work experience in various firms namely, being an accountant in a start-up that sold biodegradable and compostable partyware and assisting in sustainability reporting in two accounting firms.Her passion inspired her to create her own blog which aims to share tips related to sustainable living, which then developed into a start-up that focuses on selling a range of zero waste products to promote the vision of "Sustainable Living Made Easy" in Singapore. As the founder of The Sustainability Project, she has managed to encourage over 5,000 customers to eliminate plastic products, share zero waste lifestyle with over 4,000 individuals, give over 20,000 pieces of packaging a second life through the Zero Waste Packaging Initiative and give 120 books a second life through the pre-loved book initiative (A Million Books) which plant trees with the profits. She continues to grow her passion for the environment and strives to inspire those around her to take a step towards sustainable living.
